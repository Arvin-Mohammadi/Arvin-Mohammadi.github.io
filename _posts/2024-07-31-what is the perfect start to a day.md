Hey there Mr. Guy/Girl who is reading this! Are you having a nice day? Do you feel alive? Does the air entering your lungs soothe you? Do you feel thankful for it?

**Do you know of those days that start _just_ perfect?** I'm talking Studio Gibli-esq perfect. Have you ever experienced any? I experience them every so often and today was one of those days; so I wanted to write about it while the memory is still fresh in my mind.

**I woke up from a somewhat laughable nightmare** of not making the deadline for my final project. It felt ominous in a way; almost like a prophesy. When I woke up all my ears could hear was the sound of my own heart thumping like it had enough of being trapped in me and wanted out. I checked the date and as I slowly came back to reality, I found myself staring at the ceiling, feeling the cool breeze of the aircon on my skin. I was feeling sleepy still cause I had roughly 5 hours of sleep. I looked at the clock and it showed ```03:43```. Opting to close my eyes, I focus on the sound of the aircon. Somewhere around ```04:08``` I decided to get out of bed. I sleep on the upper bunk of the bed in my dorm. I climbed down, blindly stumbling to find my flip-flops. Of the seven people in the room, only I was awake... like always. That's why I love waking up at 4 AM so much. During that time of night it feels like you're the only person living in this world.

**I slowly turned the hot tap on, grabbing some water to throw at my face.**

> Sleep, sleep, go away,

> Come back another day.

> Eyes must open, wide and bright,

> Chase the darkness out of sight!

The sound of water shatters the silence. Then it slowly fades away with the sound of the odd trickle of water hitting the sink. Then it's back to no sounds except the aircon and the rare rustling and creaking rising from the bed when one of my roommates moves around in their sleep. I start to make myself some sort of easy breakfast. Nothing too fancy since I'm not that good at cooking, plus the sounds of dishware would most likely wake all of my roommates.

**Filling the electric kettle, I turn it on.** The switch of the kettle is broken and so I should pay attention to disconnect it when the water boils. I put my smol stovetop-coffee-maker on top of the stove, turning the heat on and letting it do its thing while I chop some fruit on my chopping board; a banana and a sour-green-apple. The slices must be thin enough to feel delicate to consume but thicc enough to feel crunchy. That's the golden rule when chopping up fruit. I don't like gooey fruits. The texture doesn't feel right. I like to feel it when I'm chewing something, rather than let it take the shape of my mouth.

**The apples are wonderfully crunchy. A gift from heaven if I believed in it.** Next up are the cheese and cucumber sandwiches, and then a sweet treat that I bought yesterday. Putting all of these into two small bowls neatly beside each other, I feel a sort of happiness that I don't experience very often. The water is boiling at that point and the coffee-maker has also finished making the coffee. I pour the espresso into my glass and put some instant coffee in my flask, filling it with boiling water for later through the day.

**I realize a certain Myles Kennedy has uploaded a new song only 10 hours ago.** I listen to the song and the world starts to fade away. Eating a light and healthy meal at 5 AM, a cup of coffee, while listening to my favorite vocalist's latest release. That's as perfect a start to a day as you can get.
