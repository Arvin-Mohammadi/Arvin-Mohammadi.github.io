## Can Someone be Afraid of EVERYTHING?

**You know how some people are afraid of exams?** Funny enough, it even has a name: "Examinophobia" or something like that. Since I'm generally an anxious person and academic tests are the perfect recipe for causing anxiety, I've experienced that kind of phobia too; at least to some extent. If I had to describe it, I would say it’s a feeling of dread and unease that starts from your stomach. It feels as if a huge spider is slowly making its way onto your body while you are unable to move or even make the slightest sound. You feel the spider’s furry, hard-shelled feet finding their way onto your skin. You feel it walking up towards your core, slowly but steadily making its progress to where your heart is.

**Did I make you uneasy?** I’m sorry. I didn’t mean to. But that’s how I feel about most things in life. Despite experiencing a shit-ton of different things throughout my 25 years of existance, I still find it absolutely terrifying to *do* anything. Even so much as my daily routines. I’ve always got to hype myself up and talk myself into doing even the simplest tasks. I get that a good number of people have a hard time with certain tasks; for example, some people have social anxiety. I get that. But I experience fear even when I want to start watching my favorite show, or when I want to start playing a game I’ve been looking forward to, the whole year, or when I want to do a simple assignment that I'm perfectly capable of handling.

![Omori](assets/img/posts/2024-07-26/1.jpg)

## What Have I Done Up Until This Point?

**I’ve always tried my best to not give in to the fear.** I usually try to calm myself down, be it with music, ASMR, or other methods. Actually, one of the reasons I started making this website is exactly to analyze myself and to understand why I’m so afraid of my own existance. I Googled this "fear of everything" to find out if there is a phobia associated with it, but I didn’t find anything convincing enough.

![Omori](assets/img/posts/2024-07-26/3.jpg)

## Where to Go From Here?

**There are certain moments... when it all goes away.** There are brief moments in life when all of that tension is lost and I feel free from everything and everyone. I made two promises to myself long ago. One was that if I’m to live... I will do it my own fucking way, not anybody else's. The other was that I shouldn’t be afraid of anything anymore because this world is just not worth it. I’ve upheld my promises to the extent of my ability, but honestly... I think I can do better. 

**I’ve experienced the feeling of freedom before.** There are days when my stomach feels just right. Days when the cold or the warmth doesn’t bother me. Days when the challenges I have ahead of me feel insignificant. Days that I can admire the view from home to work no matter how mundane. I can start on my task without any worry, get some stuff done and be pleased with my progress no matter how small. Days when cooking for myself doesn't seem scary and demanding so I don’t have to eat the stale and cold food from some random restaurant. I know the feeling. I know. But I can’t have that every day because I’m still doing something wrong. 

> My guess is that I’m still too attached to my own success. I think the best thing for me is to not have a plan. Maybe I can change
