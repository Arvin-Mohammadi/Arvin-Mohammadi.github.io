There are a number of good things about this game, namely the aesthetic, the art style, the music, the characters, and the writing. The premise of the game is built upon the weekend trip of a bunch of teenagers to a haunted island. Our protagonist, Alex, has just found out that she has a new stepbrother, and he's going to tag along with her on this trip. Not awkward at all, right? However, the way they go about figuring out this new relationship is kind of cute and also admirable. Since they're both teenagers and "at that age," they could have taken it a lot worse than they did. Alex tries to be cool with the whole situation, while Jonas goes a step further by actually trying to bond with his new sister.

I think the most immersive part of this game is the first few minutes, when there is no real story to follow, and it's just a bunch of teenagers hanging out. The first scene on the boat and the beach scene are moments I'll never forget. I also really like the flow of conversation in this game. There are never any awkward silences. There is never a moment that breaks the immersion, and I love that. The scriptwriters did a really good job of writing interesting conversations, while the developers did an even more amazing job of making the dialogue system feel dynamic and natural.

What I really liked about the ending is how they portrayed Alex's relationship with her brother. I found out that you can actually save her brother by choosing the correct options, but I'm glad I didn't. That would change the past, and I'm not really okay with that. I always think that the past should remain untouched. Maybe there's a bit too much Steins;Gate influence in my thinking, but the butterfly effect is insanely out of control, and we have no idea what our meddling will bring upon us. So yes, even if I knew what I was choosing, I would have still chosen the same path. In my version, Alex let Michael go, and that's fine by me.

> Score - 7/10
