I rewatched the Presentable Liberty let's play from Mark's old archives and to be honest I'm feeling a certain way and I'm having a very hard time finding the right words to explain it. I feel like I'm slowly losing sense of what makes me myself ... losing my grip on reality and what's really happening and what's not real. I both hate and love the feeling ... getting lost in your own mind I mean. It's the safest place I can be in, and at the same time I can have the wildest experiences. 

Am I rambling? I feel like I'm rambling. 

What I mean to say is that ... it's been a while since I'm following this trajectory of moving into more and more solitude. I really am feeling ready to move into a cabin where I can live without ever interacting with any other human being ever again. And I feel like I'll be happy there. It would just be me and my own adventures. You know I really want to make a game based on that premise. I want to explore what it feels like to be lost in your own mind ... as soon as I start making games. 

For me a good life would contain: 

- Coffee
- Internet 
- A cozy house in a faraway forest 
- Tons and tons of books
- The sound of rain 
- Infinite time to enjoy

Honestly for me that is the best life I could ever ask for. I love losing track of time ... I love getting drowned in my own mind ... I love listening to weird music and playing weird games. I mean that would get lonely after a while, but still. I'd be safe. My mind would be at ease. I probably have a lot more to say about this topic, but for today I think this'll do.
