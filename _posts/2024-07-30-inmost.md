**Where do I even start?** This game, like Katana Zero, is the perfect indie platformer game. And it is the very reason why I love indie psychological horror games so so freaking much. The story revolves around three protagonists and each one has a different game mechanic which was part of why the game never felt repetitive despite arguably having very basic combat and non-combat mechanics. But that's not what I'm focusing on because... WOULD YOU LOOK AT THE GAME? WOULD YOU JUST FUCKING LOOK AT IT? IT LOOKS SOOOOOO GORGEOUS!!! JESUS FUCKING CHRIST. 😭😭😭 

![Inmost](assets/img/posts/2024-07-30/3.webp) 

**The creators of this game are master story-tellers.** The game lets you theorize throughout the playthrough and second guess your own theories at all times, but when all comes to a close, the story will unravel itself and holy smokes was it emtotional! I could've cried for hours on end. The things I truely adore about this game are more than I could even begin to count; but the most important ones off the top of my head were the visuals, the level design, the story-telling elements, the story-line itself, the music, the sound design, and character design (both visually and personality-wise). 

**My personal favorite moments from the game** were the moments from the little girl story-line. How she coped with having an emotionally detached mother and a father who was never there... how she was living in her own little world despite everything seemingly falling apart around her. The pure innocence that was depicted in that was absolutely heart-breaking.  


![Inmost](assets/img/posts/2024-07-30/5.png) 

**I have nothing but respect for this game and its creators.** It forever will be one of my favorite games I've ever had the pleasure of experiencing.

> Score - 10/10
