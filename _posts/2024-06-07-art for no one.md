**The topic of today is *(drumroll please)* ART FOR NO ONE!!!** OK, so here's the deal my dear friend. I don't think there is a definitive definition of what "Art" is. People can come up with some sort of textbook definition as a reference to make themselves feel better, but the fact of the matter is that art is an arbitrary concept invented by humans. Art is based on feelings, and no one even understands feelings yet. People don't make art to survive; it's not a basic instinct. It's something more. Art is a deliberate choice humans make to express themselves not because they have to, but because they simply want to. And that expression can come in many shapes and forms so it's very hard to determine what counts as art and what doesn't.

**One of the aspects of art that interests me recently** is perception of art. I'm talking about a Jacob Geller video where he talks about the idea that there can be some form of art existing somewhere that no one has ever seen, except the initial creator, of course. There was this story of an incredibly talented artist who commissioned kings and queens, and half a century after he died, someone was tearing his house down when they found that on the inside of his walls, he had created a number of art pieces—pieces that were never shown to anyone or even spoken about, pieces that were created and meant to die right there without ever being appreciated by the masses, or in fact, anyone. That, to me, is fascinating because didn't we say at the start of this post that art is a form of expressing yourself? I mean, it's not exactly a solid definition or anything, but I think we can all agree that the primary goal of making art is exactly that: expressing yourself. But then comes the question: "To whom?" And that's a scary question to answer because I don't really know. Is art still art if it's not perceived by others? In the case of that talented artist's work, were his creations not art until others found out about their existence? Did they only become art the moment they were perceived? Or can you just make art for your own sake, for yourself to appreciate and no one else?

**We did say that art is a deliberate choice** and I think there is a point to be made here. If ABSOLUTELY NO ONE perceives your art, I think that must be the intention behind it. Imagine a painter who lives in the solitude in some distant forest. She sometimes goes into the nearby town to buy brushes, paper, and other art supplies but that's about all the social interaction she gets. When she comes back, she starts painting the view of the dawn. It's extremely beautiful, and she's a very talented painter, able to capture the essense of her own feelings. She's so inspired by it that she paints the view, practically everyday. It's part of her routine at this point. She never has any visitors since she doesn't have a family or any friends so no one can see her paintings. Then why exactly does she keep painting the view? In the hope that someone will eventually see the paintings? Why not show them off in the nearby town then? Maybe she wants to be immortalized here in the forest by leaving a mark behind in the physical world. But the papers will eventually rot away. Even the forest itself will eventually rot away. Maybe not in thousands of years, but in time, it too will disappear. So why does she paint with such passion every day?

**I want to try and answer that very question.** That's why I'm creating this blog/website/diary. I haven't told anyone about its existence. And virtually no one cares, even if they did know that it exists. After all, in this day and age, there are tons and tons of art that are actually way more presentable and interesting than the rambles of some rando on the internet. Why would I find the creation of this website appealing? I said that this is somewhat like hiding a treasure. But treasures are meant to be found, and I think that defeats the point of this experiment. Am I creating it because I hope that even one person will find it and take the time to understand what it is as a whole? Am I waiting for someone else to view my creation and, upon recognizing it, for it to turn into art? Is that why I'm constantly talking directly to you, the reader, in my writing? I hope not. I hope what I'm doing is not so shallow as simply waiting to be seen. What I'm aiming for here is that this website is never seen by anyone. It is buried under billions upon billions of lines of information. I want to be that girl in the woods, to be in her shoes and understand why she's doing what she's doing. Is creating art for no one a fulfilling experience, knowing that not a single soul except me knows and cares about the documentation of my life? Knowing that my legacy will rot and be forgotten before anyone has the chance to look at it?

I want to find out.
